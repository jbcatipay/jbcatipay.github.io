About Dauin

Dauin is a coastal municipality in Negros Oriental known for its diving sites and beautiful marine sanctuaries. Located southwest of Dumaguete City, Dauin serves as a gateway to Apo Island and the surrounding marine sanctuaries.

History & Location

Dauin's history is deeply rooted in its natural and cultural heritage. The town's name is derived from the Calawin tree, a majestic tree that towered over all others in the region. The Calawin tree was home to supernatural beings known as "Dawinde," who were believed to assist those in need. The town's name also reflects its agricultural past, where banana cultivation was a significant aspect of its economy. Dauin's location is strategic, with its coastal position offering access to the Bohol Sea and its proximity to Dumaguete, making it an important coastal town in Negros Oriental. The town's strategic location and rich history make it a unique and significant part of the province's heritage.

Attractions

Apo Island (Coasline and Beaches)

Apo Island is a stunning volcanic island in the Philippines, renowned for its rich marine biodiversity, beautiful dive sites, and commitment to conservation. Location and Accessibility Apo Island is located approximately 30 kilometers off the southeastern tip of Negros Island, near Dumaguete City, Negros Oriental. The island covers about 74 hectares and is part of the municipality of Dauin. To reach Apo Island, visitors typically travel to Dumaguete City and then take a boat from Dauin or Malatapay Wharf, which takes about 45 minutes.

Tourism Information and Assistance Center

The Apo Island Tourist Information Center is located in Dauin, Negros Oriental, Philippines. It is situated near the Chapel of Saint Vincent Ferrer and the Paul's Dive Shop. This center provides information and assistance for visitors to Apo Island, which is known for its stunning marine biodiversity and diving opportunities.

Dauin (Coastline & Beaches)

Dauin is a coastal municipality in the province of Negros Oriental, Philippines. It is known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life. The town is a popular destination for diving and snorkeling enthusiasts, with several marine reserves and dive sites along its coast.

Nia Dam

Dauin NIA Dam is a hidden gem in Negros Oriental, Philippines, offering a serene escape for nature lovers and tourists. The dam is a vital resource for local farmers and serves as a captivating tourist attraction. It is located in Barangay Magsaysay, Dauin, and is accessible without an entrance fee. The dam's majestic backdrop of Mt. Talinis and the oceanic view make it a picturesque spot for photography and relaxation.

St. Nicholas of Tolentino Parish Church of Dauin

St. Nicholas of Tolentino Parish of the Roman Catholic Diocese of Dumaguete was established on 1880. It is located in Brgy. Poblacion, Municipality of Dauin, Province of Negros Oriental. The Parish Fiesta is celebrated every 10th day of September in honor of St. Nicholas of Tolentino.

Food & Accommodations

BODAMCO torta

Dauin, Philippines, is known for its delicacies, particularly the BODAMCO torta, a native bread that has evolved into a refined delicacy featuring flavors like cheese and sweet coconut. This bread symbolizes Dauin's culinary heritage and is crafted through a traditional process that has been documented and innovated recently.

Kakanin

The kakanin native rice cakes/ Kakanin in Dauin, as part of the Negros Oriental cuisine, refers to traditional native delicacies made primarily from glutinous rice (malagkit) or other starches like millet and cassava, cooked with creamy coconut milk and sweetened with sugar.

Tabios

Tabios is the local name for pigeon peas (Cajanus cajan). They are a type of legume (bean or pea) rich in fiber, protein, and B vitamins. In the local cuisine of Dauin and the wider Negros region, they are often used in savory dishes, most notably in stews cooked with coconut milk, or as an ingredient in hearty soups like KBL (Kadyos, Baboy, Langka - pigeon peas, pork, and jackfruit).
